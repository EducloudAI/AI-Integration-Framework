# ğŸ“š The Five Categories of Universal Questions  
**A thinking structure that works across every layer, every role and every component**

Universal questions are organized into five categories.  
Each category strengthens a different dimension of thinking and decision making.  
Together, they create a complete cognitive framework that guides teams through clarity, quality, governance, context and value.

These categories apply everywhere in the AI Integration Framework, regardless of domain or maturity.

---

# ğŸ”¹ 1. Clarity Questions  
**Define purpose, scope and expectations**

Clarity questions ensure that teams understand what they are building, why it matters and who it is for.  
They prevent confusion, misalignment and assumptions.

Examples:

- What is the goal of this component  
- Who is the intended user or stakeholder  
- What outcome should this produce  
- What problem does this solve  
- What does success look like  

Clarity questions create shared understanding across business, technical and management roles.

---

# ğŸ”¹ 2. Quality Questions  
**Ensure consistency, robustness and reliability**

Quality questions help teams evaluate whether a component is correct, complete and trustworthy.  
They make quality a natural part of the process instead of an afterthought.

Examples:

- How do we know this is correct  
- What tests or validations are required  
- What could cause inconsistent results  
- What are the failure modes  
- How do we ensure reproducibility  

Quality questions embed craftsmanship into every decision.

---

# ğŸ”¹ 3. Governance and Risk Questions  
**Identify risks, controls and responsibilities**

Governance questions help teams think about oversight, compliance, ethics and accountability.  
They ensure that AI systems are safe, responsible and aligned with organizational standards.

Examples:

- What risks are associated with this component  
- Does this require human oversight  
- Are there compliance or ethical considerations  
- What controls are needed  
- Who is responsible for decisions  

Governance questions make responsible AI a natural part of daily work.

---

# ğŸ”¹ 4. Data and Context Questions  
**Ensure the right information is used in the right way**

Data questions help teams understand the inputs, dependencies and assumptions behind a component.  
They prevent errors caused by missing, outdated or incorrect information.

Examples:

- What data or context does this depend on  
- Is the data fresh, accurate and authorized  
- What happens if the context changes  
- What are the data limitations  
- How do we maintain data quality  

Data questions strengthen the foundation of every AI driven process.

---

# ğŸ”¹ 5. Value and Impact Questions  
**Connect the component to business outcomes**

Value questions ensure that every component contributes to measurable impact.  
They help teams think beyond execution and focus on real organizational value.

Examples:

- How does this create value  
- Which KPI does this influence  
- How will we measure success  
- What is the expected impact  
- What happens if this component fails  

Value questions align technology with strategy.
